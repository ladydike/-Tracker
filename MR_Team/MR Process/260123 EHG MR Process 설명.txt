### Meeting Purpose

Krafton HQ team provided comprehensive training on the company's Milestone Review Process to the Project Rustfire (Last Epoch 2) development team. The session covered the process framework, timeline expectations, deliverables requirements, and specific guidance for the upcoming kickoff milestone. 

### Milestone Review Process Overview

- **Core Purpose**: Validate project viability and allocate company resources (tangible and intangible) through stakeholder alignment at key development stages
- **Decision Framework**: Uses RAPID methodology where Project PD owns the plan, HQ experts provide input, and CEO makes final approval decisions
- **Key Principle**: Stakeholders are expected to disagree - CEO will choose direction after hearing all perspectives, not seeking consensus
- **Flexibility**: Milestones and timelines can adjust as projects evolve; teams should not feel overly constrained by previous commitments

### Development Stages

- **Project Kickoff**: Converts studio prototype to official project with approved budget and deliverables
- **Gameplay Validation**: Iterative stage focused on proving core fun factor and product competitiveness; typically does not advance until core gameplay loop is complete
- **Business Readiness**: Focus shifts to business viability, market strategy, and detailed production planning; publishing team takes larger role
- **Typical Timeline**: 1.5-2 years between major stages based on historical projects; milestone reviews occur every 3-6 months depending on project needs

### Process Changes (Version 2.0)

- **Advance Feedback**: Input and expectation opinions now shared with Project PD before the meeting, allowing time to assess and respond
- **Strategic Alignment**: Meeting becomes collaborative discussion rather than defensive presentation
- **Draft Submission**: First deliverable draft due 4 weeks before meeting, giving input providers adequate review time

### Milestone Review Schedule

- **T-10 weeks**: Gather with S2 team to identify required inputs; compile list for CEO approval
- **T-4 weeks**: Submit build and deliverable draft (does not need to be final, but must contain enough information for thorough assessment)
- **T-3 weeks**: Q&A session between dev team and input providers to align on project understanding
- **T-2 weeks**: Receive inputs and expectation opinions
- **T-1 week**: Submit final deliverables
- **D-Day**: Present to CEO at milestone review meeting
- **Post-meeting**: CEO decision typically communicated within 10 days

### Deliverable Requirements

Four key pillars must be included:  

**Project Overview**

- Core experience description
- Development strategy
- Budget requirements

**Expectations**

- Sales targets (worst case, base case, best case)
- Unique objectives and key validation factors
- Rationale for accepting or countering HQ feedback

**Milestone Plan**

- Key validation factors with clear methodology for proving the game is fun
- Validation methods (focus groups, internal tests, closed alphas, external mock reviews, etc.)

**Schedule**

- Tentative schedule for all future milestones through launch
- Next milestone review date must be confirmed/hard fixed

### Input Categories

**Game Review (Key Success Factors)**:  

- Build review from three distinct teams with varying perspectives
- In-depth tech review (critical for Rustfire due to engine migration)
- User test results
- Data-driven user expectations from community feedback

**Hurdle Assessment (Essential Risks)**:  

- Team sentiment and confidence surveys
- Dev team execution capability (plan vs. actual results tracking)
- Leadership management and labor risks
- Intangible resource assessment

**Market Context**:  

- S2 and creative advisor opinions
- IR team analysis for shareholder value alignment
- Corporate development division input on investment rationale (for acquired studios)

### Creative Advisor Role

- Park (company founder) serves as sole creative advisor with 20+ years industry experience
- Focus on positive feedback and constructive solutions rather than negative critique
- Available for consultation outside formal milestone process if needed

### Possible Kickoff Outcomes

Four potential results:  

**Approved**: Project proceeds with approved budget, deliverables, and development plan

**Conditional Approval**: Specific gaps must be addressed (e.g., filling key leadership positions, refining production pipeline, evaluating budget before certain phases)

**Rejected**: Project deemed not ready; CEO declines to proceed with proposal

**Dropped**: CEO determines team performance and project strength do not warrant continued resource investment (historically rare - only 1-2 cases total)

### Project Rustfire Specific Guidance

**Focus Areas**:  

**Sequel Positioning**

- Articulate what is new, what is fixed, what is preserved from Last Epoch 1
- Address tech debt, server cost burn, combat feel improvements
- Explain hybrid hero skill system changes
- Demonstrate futuristic sci-fi aesthetic differentiation from traditional ARPG settings

**Engine Migration Risk**

- Justify Unity to Unreal Engine 5 transition rationale
- Quantify cost and timeline impact
- Consider including UE5 tech demo as commitment for next milestone to demonstrate proficiency
- Address technical stability and performance considerations

**Integration and Changes**

- Document any roadmap changes since acquisition
- Clarify development delays or direction shifts
- Report integration difficulties if any

### Cultural Communication Guidance

Important nuances when interpreting CEO feedback:   

- CEO comments are opinions, not directives unless explicitly stated as "must do X"
- Silence or lack of explicit "no" does not equal approval
- Team should own decisions rather than assuming CEO casual mentions are mandates
- Western teams historically misinterpret this dynamic

### Milestone Adjustment Process

- If unavoidable circumstances require timeline changes, use formal adjustment process requiring CEO approval
- Must align with studio champion before requesting adjustment
- Process ensures proper record-keeping and prevents penalties for strategic pivots
- Historical context: Process formalized because projects previously delayed milestones without proper approval

### Evaluation Philosophy

- Project decisions not based on majority vote of input providers
- Multiple past cases where CEO approved projects despite majority negative feedback
- CEO considers whether company has expertise to properly assess the genre/market
- Common negative feedback should be addressed with prepared responses in final deliverable

### Build Submission Guidance

**Philosophy**:  

- Submit elements that cannot be communicated through documents alone
- Focus on aspects that require interactive experience to evaluate
- Early/rough builds are acceptable and expected - reviewers understand development stages
- Gray boxes and temp assets are not penalized
- For Rustfire specifically: Focus build on demonstrating Unreal Engine 5 technical foundation and capabilities

**Documentation Support**:  

- Can supplement presentation with detailed technical documents, game design docs, architecture structures
- Additional documentation welcome and will be routed to appropriate experts for assessment
- Keep presentation deck concise while providing comprehensive supporting materials

### Live Service Discussion

- Milestone Review Process only covers games under development, not live service titles
- Last Epoch 1 ongoing development funding and strategy requires separate discussion with publishing team
- Live service requires different input criteria focused on market performance vs. development validation

### Translation Requirements

- All official materials must be translated to Korean
- Translation is dev team's responsibility; coordinate with HQ translation team in advance
- Translation services provided for the meeting itself

### Timeline and Next Steps

**Current Planning**:   

- Initial discussion targeted April kickoff; revised to May to allow adequate preparation time
- May kickoff requires build finalization by end of April
- Development continues during review process - no work stoppage expected

**Milestone Cadence Guidance**:  

- Kickoff required before exceeding 20 team members
- HQ approval required before exceeding 50 team members
- Recommended 3 total milestone reviews for gameplay validation stage: kickoff, team growth milestone, vertical slice
- Exact timing between milestones flexible based on project needs

### Key Validation Factors

- Limit to 3-5 core validation factors to maintain focus
- Number of factors signals team priorities to HQ
- Too many factors raises questions about what the studio considers most critical
